Plant Defences
Revision
Quiz

Plants have two main types of defence against pathogens - chemical and physical.



Physical Defences

Many plants are covered in a thick bark, which is a tough layer of dead cells that forms a barrier against infection. Each plant cell also has a cellulose cell wall, which acts as a barrier against infection. Leaves are also covered in a waxy cuticle to protect against pathogens.



Chemical Defences

Plants such as mint and witch hazel produce antibacterial chemicals, which limit the spread of bacteria that make it past the physical defences.
