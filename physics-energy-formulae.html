Formulae

Kinetic Energy

Ek = ½mv2

Kinetic Energy = ½ x mass x speed2

  • Kinetic Energy - Joules (J)
  • Mass - Kilograms (kg)
  • Speed - Metres per second (m/s)


    • Elastic Potential Energy

    Ee = ½ke2

    Elastic Potential Energy = ½ x spring constant x extension2

  • Elastic Potential Energy - Joules (J)
  • Spring Constant - Newtons per metre(N/m)
  • Extension - Metres (m)


    • Gravitational Potential Energy

    Ep = mgh

    Gravitational Potential Energy = mass x gravitational field strength x height

  • Gravitational Potential Energy - Joules (J)
  • Mass - Kilograms (kg)
  • Gravitational Field Strength - Newtons per Kilogram (N/kg)
  • Height - Metres (m)




    • Test yourself:

  • How much kinetic energy does a 30 kg dog have when it runs at 4 m/s?
  • Robert stretches a spring with a spring constant of 4 N/m until it is extended by 60 cm. What is the elastic potential energy stored by the spring?
  • How much gravitational potential energy does a 500 g book gain when it is lifted up 1.5 m onto a shelf?


    • Work

    W = F x d

    Work Done = Force x Distance Moved

  • Work Done - Joules (J)
  • Force - Newtons (N)
  • Distance Moved - Metres (m)


    • Power

    P = W/t

    Power = Work Done / Time

  • Power - Watts (W)
  • Work Done - Joules (J)
  • Time - Seconds (s)


    • Efficiency

    Efficiency = Useful Energy Transferred / Total Energy Supplied

    Efficiency = Useful Power Transferred / Total Power Supplied

    Percentage Efficiency = Efficiency x 100

  • Energy - Joules (J)
  • Power - Watts (W)




    • Test yourself:

  • 12,000 J of energy is supplied to move a small truck a distance of 80 m. What is the size of the force applied?
  • A hair dryer transfers 48,000 J of energy in one minute. What is the power rating of the hairdryer?
  • A transformer usefully transfers 190,000 W of the 200,000 W of energy supplied to it. How efficient is the transformer?
